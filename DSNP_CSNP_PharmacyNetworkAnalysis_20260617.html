Executive Summary — Answer to Sir Salim’s Question
✓ Finding: CHG Can Meet All Member Pharmacy Needs Without CVS
Non-CVS retail pharmacy access is identical to the Full Network for both D-SNP and C-SNP. The Quest Analytics access analysis (10 miles / 30 minutes, estimated driving distance) shows that removing CVS from the network does not reduce member access — the 216 Non-CVS pharmacies serve exactly the same members the full 316-pharmacy network serves. The 1,454 D-SNP and 69 C-SNP locations that lack CVS access are also served by non-CVS providers, confirming no dependency.
⚠ CVS-Only Coverage Is Materially Lower
With CVS as the sole source, D-SNP access falls to 81.5% (6,399 / 7,853) and C-SNP to 73.6% (192 / 261). This confirms CVS cannot serve the full network independently — but the inverse question (can non-CVS serve the full network?) answers Yes.

Conclusions & Recommendations for Contract Administration
1. No Material Member Access Impact from CVS Removal
Both D-SNP (98.2%) and C-SNP (98.9%) achieve identical access rates whether using the full pharmacy network or the non-CVS-only subset. The 143 D-SNP and 3 C-SNP members who lack access do so under all scenarios — their gap is geographic, not CVS-specific.
2. Non-CVS Network Is Sufficient and Self-Standing
With 201 Retail Chain Non-CVS providers and 216 total Non-CVS pharmacies, the non-CVS network independently meets the 10-mile / 30-minute CMS access standard for 98%+ of D-SNP and C-SNP populations.
3. Noteworthy: CVS Cannot Stand Alone
If CVS were the only pharmacy source, 1,454 D-SNP members (18.5%) and 69 C-SNP members (26.4%) would lose access. This data may be useful context for contract negotiation positioning.
4. Residual 1.1–1.8% Access Gap Exists Independent of CVS
143 D-SNP and 3 C-SNP member locations lack any pharmacy within 10 miles / 30 minutes under all provider configurations. These are likely rural/fringe ZIP areas. A separate geographic access remediation strategy (e.g., mail-order pharmacy, 90-day supply) may be warranted.
