ECM Enrolled Population Impact — Existing POF Logic vs. Algorithm POF Logic

JAG requested a defensible count of ECM Enrolled members who would lose ECM eligibility entirely under the revised algorithm POF logic. The team built and cross-validated a BASELINE ECM Enrolled population of 22,864 members, then compared each member against 16 existing POF tables and 16 algorithm POF tables (ECMPOF_CHILD_HOMELESS confirmed sunset/empty, excluded from both sides for a clean 1:1 comparison). Result: 1,381 members (6.0%) fully lose ECM POF qualification, and a further 831 (3.6%) lose one or more POFs but retain at least one, remaining ECM Enrolled.

What Changed In The Algorithm Logic
  • Program Eligibility gate added. SMHS (MHPs) OR DMC-ODS OR DMC eligibility now required as a precondition, not previously enforced in the existing logic.
  • Social Complexity gate added (Adult). At least one complex social factor now required in addition to Program Eligibility, per DHCS CalAIM ECM Policy Guide pg. 25.
  • Utilization/Risk gate added (Adult). High-risk overdose/suicide or institutionalization, or 2+ ED/hospitalization events in 12 months, now required as a third independent condition.
  • Children/Youth exemption not fully applied. DHCS explicitly exempts Children/Youth SMH/SUD from the Social Complexity and Utilization/Risk gates (pg. 25–26). The current Child algorithm script still applies both — an open, documented gap, not corrected per prior explicit decision.
  • ECMPOF_CHILD_HOMELESS retired. Table confirmed sunset and empty; excluded from both sides of the comparison rather than silently returning zero rows.
  • Comparison scoped to full loss, not partial. "Disenrollment" defined strictly as zero matches across all 16 algorithm tables, not merely absent from one — members who requalify under a different POF are not counted as disenrolled.
Data Quality Checks Passed
  • Concurrent provider assignments: 0 members with more than one currently-active MemberProviderAssigment row — Kathryn's "one provider at a time" rule holds against live data.
  • Demographic match: 0 BASELINE members failed to resolve to a member record — no orphaned rows in the Excel refresh.
  • Duplicate rows: memid + EnrollEffDate deduplication applied via ROWNUMBER_Enrolled = 1 before the final count.
  • Delivered: 4-worksheet Excel OLE DB/ODBC workbook (Sanity Summary, BASELINE, Full Disenrollment, Partial Retained) sent to Kathryn Madrid and Yousaf Farook for review.
